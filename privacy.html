Privacy Policy

Bastion exists to give you control over what reaches your devices — so we hold ourselves to the same standard with your own information. This policy explains exactly what we collect, why, how long we keep it, and the data we access when you sign in with Google.

01 Who we are

Bastion ("Bastion", "we", "us" or "our") provides DNS-based content filtering for families and teams. We let you block categories of content and specific sites across the phones, tablets and computers you manage, and review a short rolling history of activity from a single dashboard.

This Privacy Policy applies to the Bastion website at bastion.codeunbound.dev, the Bastion dashboard, and the filtering service (together, the "Service"). By creating an account or using the Service, you agree to the practices described here.

02 Information we collect

We collect only what we need to run the Service, bill you correctly, and show you a useful dashboard. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not build advertising profiles.

Account information
Your name and email address, obtained when you sign in (including via Google), plus authentication identifiers needed to keep you signed in securely.
Device & profile data
The devices you add (a label you choose, platform such as iOS or Android, and a private per-device DNS identifier) and the filtering rules and categories you configure.
Filtering activity
A rolling, time-limited record of DNS queries made by your managed devices — the domain requested, whether it was allowed or blocked, and a timestamp — so we can show your dashboard history and reports.
Billing information
Plan, billing status and transaction references. Card and payment details are handled by our payment processor; we do not store full card numbers.
Technical & usage data
Standard log data such as IP address, browser type and timestamps, used to keep the Service secure, reliable and free of abuse.

Filtering activity is generated by the devices you manage on your account, not by browsing you do elsewhere. It is tied to the device identifiers you set up in Bastion.

03 Google sign-in & Google user data

Bastion offers "Sign in with Google" so you can create and access your account without managing a separate password. When you choose this option, Google asks for your permission and then shares a limited set of profile information with us.

What we request from Google

  • Email address — used as your account identifier and to send essential service communications.
  • Basic profile information (name and, if available, profile picture) — used to personalise your dashboard.

We request the minimum scopes necessary for sign-in and account identification. We do not request access to your Gmail, Google Drive, Contacts, Calendar or any other Google service data.

Google API Services — Limited Use disclosure

Bastion's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We use Google user data only to provide and improve the sign-in and account features you have requested. We do not use it for advertising, we do not sell it, and we do not transfer it to others except as needed to provide the Service, to comply with applicable law, or as part of a merger or acquisition with appropriate protections. We do not allow humans to read this data unless we have your consent, it is necessary for security or to comply with law, or the data has been aggregated and anonymised.

Revoking access

You can disconnect Bastion from your Google Account at any time at myaccount.google.com/permissions. Doing so stops future access to your Google profile data; to also delete the data already held in your Bastion account, see Your rights & choices.

04 How we use information

  • To create and secure your account and authenticate you when you sign in.
  • To deliver the core Service — applying your filtering rules to your managed devices.
  • To show your dashboard, including the rolling history and per-device activity reports.
  • To process payments, manage subscriptions and send essential account or billing notices.
  • To maintain security, prevent abuse, debug issues and improve the reliability of the Service.
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms.

05 How we share information

We do not sell your personal information. We share it only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service providers who process data on our behalf — for example cloud hosting, payment processing and email delivery — under contracts that limit them to providing those services.
  • Within your organisation — for business and enterprise plans, account administrators can see the devices and activity reports for the accounts they manage.
  • Legal & safety — where required by valid legal process, or to protect the rights, safety and security of our users, the public or Bastion.
  • Business transfers — if Bastion is involved in a merger, acquisition or sale of assets, with notice and continued protection of your data.

06 Data retention

We keep information only as long as it serves the purpose it was collected for:

  • Filtering activity is kept on a rolling 7-day window and is automatically and permanently deleted as it ages out — we hold raw history for 7 days, then it's gone. Business and Enterprise plans can extend this window going forward (for example 30 or 90 days); a longer window applies from the day you enable it and does not recover data already deleted.
  • Account, device and billing data is retained while your account is active and for a limited period afterward to meet legal, tax and accounting obligations.
  • When you delete your account, we delete or anonymise your personal data except where we must retain it by law.

07 How we protect data

We protect your information with encryption in transit, access controls that limit who can reach your data, per-device isolation so accounts and histories don't mix, and routine monitoring for abuse. Enterprise customers can run on a dedicated, isolated instance for full traffic separation. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect your data and to respond quickly if something goes wrong.

08 Your rights & choices

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, export or delete your personal information, and to object to or restrict certain processing. You can:

  • View and update your account and device details from the dashboard.
  • Request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data by emailing us.
  • Disconnect Google sign-in from your Google Account permissions page.
  • Close your account, which removes your personal data subject to the retention rules above.

To make a request, contact us at support@bastion.codeunbound.dev. We will respond within a reasonable time and as required by applicable law.

09 Children's privacy

Bastion is designed for parents, guardians and organisations to manage devices — including those used by children — but accounts are intended to be created and operated by adults. The Service is not directed to children for the purpose of collecting their personal information directly, and we do not knowingly create accounts for children. If you believe a child has provided us personal information without appropriate consent, please contact us so we can remove it.

10 Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the Service evolves or as laws change. When we make material changes, we will revise the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you by email or in the dashboard. Your continued use of the Service after an update means you accept the revised policy.
